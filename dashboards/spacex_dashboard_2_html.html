Landing Equipment Impact

Both grid fins and legs dramatically increase landing success rates, from below 10% to over 80%.

Success Rate by Orbit Type

SSO orbit missions have 100% success rate, followed by VLEO (85.7%) and LEO (71.4%). GTO has the lowest success rate at 51.9%. VLEO missions carry the heaviest payloads by far (avg: 15,429 kg).

Launch Site Analysis

Launch Site Success Rates

KSC LC 39A has the highest success rate (77.3%), followed closely by VAFB SLC 4E (76.9%). CCSFS SLC 40 has the lowest rate (60%).

Payload Mass by Launch Site

KSC LC 39A handles the heaviest payloads on average (7,645 kg), while VAFB SLC 4E has a lower maximum capacity (9,600 kg) compared to other sites (15,600 kg). The data confirms that VAFB-SLC doesn't launch very heavy payloads (>10,000 kg) compared to other sites.

Orbit Type Analysis

Success Rate by Orbit Type

SSO orbit missions have 100% success rate, followed by VLEO (85.7%) and LEO (71.4%). GTO has the lowest success rate at 51.9%.

Payload Mass by Orbit Type

VLEO missions carry the heaviest payloads by far (avg: 15,429 kg), followed by Polar Orbit (PO) missions (avg: 7,584 kg). With heavy payloads the successful landing rate is higher for Polar, LEO and ISS orbits.

Time Trend Analysis

Yearly Landing Success Trend

Success rates improved significantly from 2013 onwards, reaching over 80% by 2017 and peaking at 90% in 2019. The data confirms that "the success rate since 2013 kept increasing till 2020".

Data Type Conversion

  • Boolean values in GridFins, Reused, and Legs columns were converted from string format ("True"/"False") to numeric values (1/0).
  • All numeric columns (FlightNumber, PayloadMass, etc.) were converted to the same data type (float64) for compatibility with machine learning algorithms.
  • All one-hot encoded columns were created as numeric (float64) type with binary values (0/1).
